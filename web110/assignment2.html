Two other things I've learned from this lesson on the Wikiversity reading was the existence of ActiveX, I've never heard of this software framework until recently (maybe I have heard of it before but have never known the use of it, plus it's deprecated now so that's probably why I don't know it well because it's rarely used nowadays), and for it's time used to increase functionality for web browsers by creating plugins or programs for the browser.

The other topic I've learned from the reading was the existence of Silverlight by Microsoft. It is also deprecated just like ActiveX and was designed for writing and running rich internet applications, which is similar to Adobe Flash which focuses on graphics, multimedia, and streaming media.
